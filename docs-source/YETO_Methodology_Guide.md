## 1. Introduction

The Yemen Economic Transparency Observatory (YETO) is a comprehensive platform designed to provide verifiable, evidence-based economic intelligence on Yemen. This methodology guide documents our data collection, verification, and presentation standards.

### 1.1 Mission Statement

YETO aims to enhance economic transparency in Yemen by:
- Aggregating data from multiple credible sources
- Applying rigorous verification standards
- Presenting information with full source attribution
- Enabling informed decision-making for policymakers, researchers, and stakeholders

### 1.2 Scope

This platform covers 16 economic sectors:
1. Macroeconomy
2. Currency & Exchange
3. Banking & Finance
4. Agriculture & Food
5. Trade & Commerce
6. Energy & Fuel
7. Labor & Employment
8. Humanitarian & Aid
9. Infrastructure
10. Health
11. Education
12. Telecommunications
13. Transportation
14. Water & Sanitation
15. Poverty & Welfare
16. Governance & Institutions

## 2. Data Source Classification

### 2.1 Tier System

YETO employs a four-tier classification system for data sources:

| Tier | Category | Description | Examples |
|------|----------|-------------|----------|
| T0 | Primary Official | Direct government/central bank releases | CBY bulletins, Ministry reports |
| T1 | International Organizations | UN agencies, World Bank, IMF | WDI, WEO, OCHA reports |
| T2 | Research & Academic | Peer-reviewed studies, think tanks | ACAPS, Chatham House |
| T3 | Media & Field Reports | News outlets, NGO field reports | Reuters, Al Jazeera, local NGOs |

### 2.2 Source Verification Process

Each data point undergoes:
1. **Origin Verification**: Confirming the source is authentic
2. **Cross-Reference Check**: Comparing with alternative sources when available
3. **Temporal Validation**: Ensuring data currency and relevance
4. **Methodology Review**: Understanding how the original data was collected

## 3. Confidence Grading System

### 3.1 Grade Definitions

| Grade | Label | Criteria |
|-------|-------|----------|
| A | High Confidence | Official source, multiple verification, recent data |
| B | Moderate Confidence | Credible source, limited verification, reasonably current |
| C | Low Confidence | Single source, unverified, or older data |
| D | Estimate/Proxy | Calculated estimate or proxy indicator |
| E | Contested | Multiple conflicting sources, politically sensitive |

### 3.2 Grade Assignment Process

Grades are assigned based on:
- Source tier classification
- Number of corroborating sources
- Data recency (within 6 months = higher grade)
- Methodology transparency

## 4. Data Collection Procedures

### 4.1 Automated Collection

- API integrations with World Bank, IMF, UN agencies
- Scheduled scraping of official government portals
- RSS feed monitoring for news and reports

### 4.2 Manual Collection

- Document review and data extraction
- Expert interviews and consultations
- Field report analysis

### 4.3 Update Frequency

| Data Type | Update Frequency |
|-----------|------------------|
| Exchange rates | Daily |
| Market prices | Weekly |
| Economic indicators | Monthly/Quarterly |
| Structural data | Annually |

---

## 5. Quality Assurance

### 5.1 Data Validation

- Automated range checks and outlier detection
- Historical trend analysis
- Cross-source reconciliation

### 5.2 Error Correction

When errors are identified:
1. Issue flagged and documented
2. Source contacted for clarification
3. Correction applied with timestamp
4. Correction note added to data record

### 5.3 Audit Trail

All data modifications are logged with:
- Timestamp
- User/system identifier
- Previous value
- New value
- Reason for change

---

## 6. Data Presentation Standards

### 6.1 Visualization Guidelines

- Clear axis labels and units
- Source attribution on all charts
- Confidence grade displayed
- Last update timestamp shown

### 6.2 Accessibility

- Bilingual support (Arabic/English)
- Screen reader compatibility
- Mobile-responsive design
- Data export options (CSV, JSON, Excel)

---

## 7. Limitations and Disclaimers

### 7.1 Known Limitations

- Data gaps due to conflict conditions
- Dual authority reporting challenges
- Currency conversion complexities
- Delayed official statistics

### 7.2 Usage Guidelines

- Data should be cited with source attribution
- Confidence grades should be considered in analysis
- Historical data may be revised as new information emerges
- Platform data is for informational purposes only
