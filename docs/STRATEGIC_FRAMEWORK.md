# YETO Strategic Excellence Framework

## Vision Statement

YETO (Yemen Economic Transparency Observatory) shall be the definitive source of economic intelligence for Yemen, meeting or exceeding the analytical standards of the International Monetary Fund, World Bank, and leading central banks worldwide. The platform will serve as an indispensable resource for donors, policymakers, researchers, financial institutions, and the international community.

---

## Core Principles

### 1. Methodological Rigor

All economic analysis on YETO follows established international standards:

| Framework | Application | Standard |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| IMF SDDS | Data dissemination | Special Data Dissemination Standard |
| World Bank DQAF | Data quality | Data Quality Assessment Framework |
| BIS Guidelines | Banking statistics | Bank for International Settlements |
| ILO Standards | Labor statistics | International Labour Organization |
| FAO Guidelines | Food security | Food and Agriculture Organization |
| OECD Principles | Governance data | Organisation for Economic Co-operation |

### 2. Dual-Regime Coverage

YETO uniquely tracks both economic systems operating in Yemen:

**Internationally Recognized Government (IRG) - Aden**
- Central Bank of Yemen - Aden
- New Yemeni Rial (post-2016)
- Floating exchange rate regime
- Saudi-backed monetary support

**De Facto Authority (DFA) - Sana'a**
- Central Bank of Yemen - Sana'a
- Old Yemeni Rial (pre-2016 notes)
- Controlled exchange rate regime
- Import substitution policies

### 3. Data Provenance & Confidence

Every data point carries:
- **Source Attribution**: Primary source identification
- **Confidence Rating**: A (verified) to E (estimate)
- **Timestamp**: Collection and publication dates
- **Methodology Notes**: How the data was derived
- **Regime Tag**: Which authority the data pertains to

## Analytical Frameworks

### Macroeconomic Analysis

**GDP Estimation Methodology**
- Production approach: Sectoral output aggregation
- Expenditure approach: C + I + G + (X-M)
- Income approach: Wages + Profits + Rents + Interest
- Cross-validation with satellite imagery (nighttime lights)
- Informal economy adjustment factors

**Inflation Measurement**
- Consumer Price Index (CPI) tracking
- Producer Price Index (PPI) monitoring
- Food price indices (WFP methodology)
- Regional price differentials (Aden vs Sana'a)
- Hedonic adjustments for quality changes

**Exchange Rate Analysis**
- Official rate tracking (CBY Aden, CBY Sana'a)
- Parallel market monitoring (money changers)
- Real effective exchange rate (REER) calculation
- Purchasing power parity (PPP) estimates
- Currency spread analysis (North-South differential)

### Sectoral Analysis

**Banking Sector**
- Capital adequacy ratios (Basel standards)
- Non-performing loan ratios
- Liquidity coverage ratios
- Return on assets/equity
- Deposit concentration analysis

**Trade Sector**
- Import/export composition
- Trade balance trends
- Terms of trade calculations
- Partner country analysis
- Sanctions impact assessment

**Energy Sector**
- Oil production volumes
- Fuel price monitoring
- Electricity generation capacity
- Energy import dependency
- Renewable potential assessment

**Humanitarian Economy**
- Aid flow tracking (OCHA FTS)
- Humanitarian funding gaps
- Cash transfer programs
- Food assistance coverage
- Protection needs assessment
