# HSA Group Comprehensive Data

## Company Overview
- **Full Name:** Hayel Saeed Anam Group (HSA Group)
- **Arabic Name:** مجموعة هائل سعيد أنعم
- **Founded:** 1938 in Aden, Yemen
- **Founders:** Four brothers from the Saeed Anam family
- **Headquarters:** Yemen (with global operations)
- **Employees in Yemen:** 20,000+
- **Status:** Yemen's largest private company

## Key Timeline Events (2010-2025)

### 2010-2014: Pre-Conflict Growth
- Continued expansion of food processing operations
- Major investments in flour mills and sugar refining

### 2015-2016: Conflict Impact
- Yemen conflict escalates (March 2015)
- 2016: Some HSA assets in Taiz were bombed
- Operations slowed but continued

### 2017-2019: Resilience
- Maintained operations despite port blockades
- Plants operated on 5-10 day raw material inventory
- Continued partnership with WFP

### 2020: IFC Project Initiation
- September 30, 2020: IFC project disclosure for HSA Foods Yemen
- Project Number: 43466
- Proposed investment: $75 million financing package

### 2021: Major IFC Partnership
- **August 10, 2021:** IFC announces $75M financing package
  - $55M from IFC's own account
  - $20M syndicated loan from FMO (Dutch Development Bank)
  - 50% first-loss guarantee from IDA Private Sector Window
- First IFC investment in Yemen's agribusiness in 10+ years
- **December 10, 2020:** IFC Board approval

### 2022: Ukraine Crisis Impact
- War in Ukraine impacts wheat supply (Ukraine/Russia = 30% global wheat)
- Wheat prices increased 50%+
- HSA called for emergency financial mechanisms
- IFC story published: "Yemeni Company Feeds Millions Despite Relentless Challenges"
- Yasmin Mokhtar (CFO) spoke at World Bank Fragility Forum (March 2022)

### 2023: Continued Operations
- Cross-sector partnerships for food security
- Devex feature: "Food security for tomorrow"

### 2024-2025: Ongoing Impact
- Environmental and Social Policy published (January 2026)
- Continued WFP partnership
- Ongoing food security operations
