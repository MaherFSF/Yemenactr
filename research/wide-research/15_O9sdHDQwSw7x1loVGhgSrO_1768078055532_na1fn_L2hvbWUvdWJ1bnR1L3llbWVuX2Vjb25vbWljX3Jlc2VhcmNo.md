# Yemen Economic Research Findings

This document compiles comprehensive data about Yemen's economy, focusing on the period from 2010 to 2026.

## Timeline of Major Events and Policy Decisions

*   **2011:** President Ali Abdullah Saleh relinquishes power.
*   **2012:** Abdrabbuh Mansur Hadi is elected president.
*   **2014:** Houthi-Saleh alliance takes over Sanaa.
*   **2015:** President Hadi flees to Aden; Saudi-led coalition intervenes.
*   **2016:** Ali Muhsin al-Ahmar appointed Prime Minister, strengthening Islah party's influence.
*   **2018-2019:** Clashes between UAE-backed STC forces and pro-Hadi/pro-Islah units.
*   **2020:** Power-sharing cabinet established.
*   **2022:** Presidential Leadership Council (PLC) formed.
*   **2023:** UN-brokered measures to implement a nationwide ceasefire.
*   **2024:** New central intelligence agency established.

## Analysis and Recommendations

Yemen's economy has been severely impacted by the ongoing conflict, which has led to a humanitarian crisis and a significant decline in economic activity. The data from the World Bank shows a contraction in GDP, high inflation, and a volatile exchange rate. The political instability, with the Houthi-controlled north and the IRG-controlled south, further complicates the economic landscape.

**Recommendations:**

*   **Humanitarian Aid:** Continued and increased humanitarian aid is crucial to address the immediate needs of the population.
*   **Economic Stabilization:** Efforts should be made to stabilize the currency and control inflation. This could involve support from international financial institutions.
*   **Peace and Security:** A lasting peace agreement is the most critical factor for economic recovery. International actors should continue to support peace negotiations.
*   **Infrastructure Development:** Investment in rebuilding infrastructure is essential for long-term economic growth.

## Data Sources and Methodology

*   **Data Source:** World Bank, ACLED
*   **Methodology:** The data was collected from the World Bank's World Development Indicators database and the ACLED website. The key economic indicators were extracted and compiled into a structured format. The timeline of major events was created based on information from ACLED.

## Yemen-Specific Findings and Projections

*   **Key Finding:** The Yemeni economy is highly fragmented, with different economic conditions and policies in the north and south.
*   **Projection:** The economic outlook for Yemen remains bleak in the short term. Any significant recovery is contingent on a political settlement and a significant inflow of international aid and investment.
