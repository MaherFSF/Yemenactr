## Key Findings

### The Economy - The Yemen Review, July-September 2025

*   **Food Security:**
    *   62% of households lacked adequate access to food in August 2025.
    *   Food insecurity is higher in government-controlled areas (64%) than in Houthi-controlled areas (62%).
    *   The price of a Minimum Food Basket (MFB) decreased by 34% month-on-month in government-controlled areas due to the appreciation of the rial.
    *   Food imports declined by 20% at Houthi-controlled Red Sea ports.
    *   18.1 million people are estimated to face high levels of acute food insecurity between September 2025 and February 2026.
*   **Government's Fiscal Crisis:**
    *   The government has been operating without an official budget since 2019.
    *   The government's direct borrowing from the central bank reached YR6.9 trillion by the end of June 2025.
    *   Saudi financial support of US$368 million was announced but not yet delivered.

### A Last Chance to Tackle Corruption - The Yemen Review, October-December 2024

*   **Corruption:**
    *   Leaked official reports detailed corruption in the telecoms sector, lavish spending at diplomatic missions, and the establishment of a US$2.8 billion commercial entity by the state oil firm PetroMasila.
    *   The electricity sector cost US$2.27 billion to manage in 2022 due to corruption.
    *   Saudi Arabia has tightened funding due to concerns over misspending in Aden.
