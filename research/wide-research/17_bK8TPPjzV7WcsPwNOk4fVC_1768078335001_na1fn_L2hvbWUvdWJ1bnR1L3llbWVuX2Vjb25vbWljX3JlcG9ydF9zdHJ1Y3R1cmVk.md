## 2. Major Events and Policy Decisions by Year (2010-2026)

* **2010:** Government offensive against separatists in southern Shabwa province.
* **2011:** President Saleh agrees to hand over power to his deputy, Abdrabbuh Mansour Hadi. A unity government is formed.
* **2012:** Abdrabbuh Mansour Hadi is inaugurated as president.
* **2014:** Houthis seize control of most of Sanaa.
* **2015:** Civil war breaks out. A Saudi-led coalition launches air strikes against Houthi targets.
* **2018:** Southern Yemeni separatists seize control of Aden.
* **2019:** Separatists and government sign a power-sharing agreement.

## 3. Analysis and Recommendations

The collected data reveals an economy in deep crisis, severely impacted by political instability and prolonged conflict. The significant drop in GDP growth in 2011 and 2015 directly correlates with the Arab Spring and the onset of the civil war, respectively. High inflation and a sharply depreciating currency have devastated the purchasing power of ordinary Yemenis, worsening the ongoing humanitarian crisis. The country's heavy reliance on oil exports, a sector that has seen production disruptions, makes the economy vulnerable to global energy price fluctuations.

Recommendations for a sustainable recovery path include:

*   **Conflict Resolution:** A lasting peace is the most critical factor for economic recovery. Without political stability, any development efforts are likely to fail.
*   **Economic Diversification:** Yemen must reduce its dependency on oil and develop other sectors such as agriculture, fisheries, and manufacturing. This will create a more resilient and inclusive economy.
*   **Institutional Reform:** Strengthening governance, combating corruption, and establishing a stable regulatory framework are essential to attract investment and foster private sector growth.
*   **Humanitarian and Development Aid:** Continued international support is vital not only for immediate humanitarian needs but also for long-term reconstruction and development projects.
*   **Energy Sector Reform:** A transition to a more sustainable and equitable energy system, including decentralized renewable energy solutions, can help address the severe energy crisis and support economic recovery.
