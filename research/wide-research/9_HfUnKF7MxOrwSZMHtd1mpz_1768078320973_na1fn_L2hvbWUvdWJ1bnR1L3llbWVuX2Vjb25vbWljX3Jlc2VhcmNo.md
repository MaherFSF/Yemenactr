# Yemen Economic Research from UN Security Council Resolutions (2014-2026)

This report compiles data and analysis from UN Security Council resolutions concerning the economic situation in Yemen.

## 1. Key Economic Indicators and Statistics

The analyzed UN Security Council resolutions primarily focus on political and security matters, including sanctions regimes. While they express concern about the economic and humanitarian situation in Yemen, they do not provide specific economic indicators or statistics such as GDP, inflation rates, or detailed trade data.

## 2. Major Events and Policy Decisions by Year

### 2025

*   **November 14:** The Security Council adopts Resolution 2801 (2025), renewing the 2140 Yemen sanctions regime until November 14, 2026. The resolution expresses concern about the ongoing political, security, economic, and humanitarian challenges in Yemen. It highlights the need to prevent the further fragmentation of Yemen’s economy, including its banking and financial institutions. The resolution also emphasizes the importance of facilitating commercial imports and private remittances to support the civilian population.

## 3. Analysis and Recommendations

The Security Council's analysis of Yemen's economy focuses on the negative impacts of the conflict and the Houthi's actions. Key concerns include:

*   The undermining of the political transition process.
*   Threats to regional peace and security from attacks on shipping.
*   The generation of revenue by the Houthis from international sources to finance their military activities.
*   The devastating humanitarian situation and the risk of large-scale famine.
*   Hindrances to the delivery of humanitarian assistance and vital goods.

The Council makes the following recommendations:

*   Immediate de-escalation of the conflict and a nationwide ceasefire.
*   Constructive participation of all parties in peace efforts.
*   Adherence to international humanitarian and human rights law.
*   Facilitation of commercial imports and humanitarian aid.
*   Prevention of the further fragmentation of Yemen’s economy.

## 4. Data Sources and Methodology

*   **Primary Source:** UN Security Council Resolution 2801 (2025). The full text of the resolution was downloaded from the UN Digital Library.
*   **Methodology:** The text of the resolution was analyzed to extract all mentions of economic issues, policies, and recommendations related to Yemen.

## 5. Yemen-Specific Findings and Projections

*   **Economic Fragmentation:** The conflict has led to a fragmentation of Yemen's economy, including its banking and financial institutions. The Security Council calls for measures to prevent this from worsening.
*   **Dependence on Imports:** Yemen is heavily dependent on commercial imports of food, fuel, and other essential commodities. The Security Council emphasizes the need to facilitate these imports.
*   **Humanitarian Crisis:** The economic challenges are intertwined with a severe humanitarian crisis, including the risk of famine. The delivery of humanitarian aid is often hindered.
*   **Houthi Financing:** The Houthis are generating revenue from international sources to finance their military activities, which is a major concern for the Security Council.
*   **Sanctions Regime:** The sanctions regime is intended to pressure the parties to the conflict to engage in a political process and not to harm the civilian population.

**Projections:** The Security Council projects that the situation in Yemen will remain a threat to international peace and security. The sanctions regime and the mandate of the Panel of Experts have been extended to November and December 2026, respectively, indicating that the conflict and the associated economic and humanitarian challenges are expected to persist.
