# World Bank Yemen Economic Monitor - Fall 2025

**Source:** World Bank Press Release, November 17, 2025
**Title:** Navigating Increased Hardship and Growing Economic Fragmentation
**URL:** https://www.worldbank.org/en/news/press-release/2025/11/17/economic-hardship-deepens-in-yemen

## Key Findings

### GDP Projection
- Real GDP projected to decline by 1.5% in 2025
- Contraction threatens to further heighten food insecurity

### Exchange Rate Crisis (2025)
- July 2025: Rial reached all-time low of YER 2,905 per USD (Aden market)
- Early August 2025: Stabilization measures brought rate back to YER 1,676 per USD
- Marked depreciation eroded purchasing power

### Inflation Impact
- June 2025: Basic food basket price 26% higher than year earlier
- Households facing mounting economic hardship
- Purchasing power severely eroded

### Government Finances (IRG)
- Revenues fell 30% compared to same period last year
- Government forced to implement spending cuts
- Public services disrupted
- Civil servant salary payments delayed

### Houthi-Controlled Areas
- Airstrikes on key ports restricting imports
- Persistent liquidity shortages
- Access to essential goods limited
- Banks relocating from Sana'a to Aden to avoid sanctions

### Humanitarian Funding Crisis
- September 2025: Only 19% of USD 2.5 billion UN Humanitarian Response Plan funded
- Lowest funding level in over a decade
- International assistance declining

### Food Security
- Over 60% of households in both IRG and Houthi areas report inadequate food consumption
- Many resorting to negative coping mechanisms including begging

### Key Challenges
1. Continued blockade on oil exports
2. Limited foreign exchange reserves
3. Declining donor support
4. Constrained ability to sustain essential services
5. Limited capacity to finance imports

### Recommended Measures
- Strengthen public financial management
- Enhance revenue mobilization
- Protect basic services, especially electricity sector
- Preserve currency stability
- Safeguard banking sector
- Implement phased reforms from IRG's "Plan for Economic Developments and Urgent Priorities" (December 2024)

### Quote from Dina Abu-Ghaida (WB Country Manager for Yemen)
"Economic stabilization in Yemen depends on strengthening the systems that keep services running and livelihoods protected. Restoring confidence requires effective institutions, predictable financing, and progress toward peace to allow economic activity to resume and recovery to take hold."
