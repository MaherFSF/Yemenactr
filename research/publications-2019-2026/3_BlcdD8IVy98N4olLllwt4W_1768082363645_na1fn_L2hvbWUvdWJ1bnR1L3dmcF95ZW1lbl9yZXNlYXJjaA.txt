WFP Yemen Research Findings



**Publication:** WFP Yemen Annual Country Report 2024
**Publication Date:** 2025 (from the report content)
**URL:** https://docs.wfp.org/api/documents/WFP-0000165370/download/
**Key Findings:**
* Assisted 8.6 million people in Yemen in 2024.
* A deepening economic crisis and crumbling public services depleted household assets, resilience, and livelihoods.
* 18.2 million people needed humanitarian assistance, with 16.8 million people (almost half the population) food insecure.
* Resumed regular food assistance in SBA-controlled areas in November 2024 after an eight-month pause.
* Suspended malnutrition prevention programme in January 2024 due to funding shortfalls, leaving 2.4 million at-risk children and pregnant and breastfeeding women and girls without support.
**Economic Indicators Mentioned:**
* Economic crisis
* Exchange rate


**Publication:** WFP Yemen Annual Country Report 2023
**Publication Date:** 2024
**URL:** https://docs.wfp.org/api/documents/WFP-0000157774/download/
**Key Findings:**
* Assisted 15.3 million people in Yemen in 2023.
* 21.6 million people needed humanitarian assistance, with half of the population facing acute hunger.
* A USD 1.66 billion funding gap forced WFP to reduce the quantity and frequency of assistance.
* Paused the General Food Assistance programme in SBA-controlled areas, affecting 9.5 million people.
* Suspended malnutrition prevention programme, leaving more than two million children and pregnant and breastfeeding women and girls at risk.
**Economic Indicators Mentioned:**
* Economic deterioration
* Inflation
* Exchange rate


**Publication:** WFP Yemen Annual Country Report 2022
**Publication Date:** 2023
**URL:** https://wfp.tind.io/record/127176/files/ELR%201572.pdf
**Key Findings:**
* Assisted 15.3 million people in Yemen in 2022.
* 17 million people faced acute hunger (IPC phases 3 and above).
* 23.4 million people, more than 70 percent of the population, were estimated to need some form of humanitarian assistance.
* Funding shortfalls forced WFP to reduce general food assistance (GFA) rations, fully suspend its resilience and livelihoods-activity for several months, partially suspend nutrition interventions, and reduce the scope of a planned school feeding expansion.
* An updated Integrated Food Security Phase Classification (IPC) analysis showed no pockets of famine-like conditions (IPC Phase 5) for the first time since 2019.
**Economic Indicators Mentioned:**
* Economic marginalization
* Inflation
* Exchange rate
* Global food and fuel price growth


**Publication:** WFP Yemen Annual Country Report 2021
**Publication Date:** 2022
**URL:** https://wfp.tind.io/record/127292/files/ELR1757.pdf
**Key Findings:**
* Assisted 15.5 million people in Yemen in 2021.
* 20.7 million people, 66 percent of the total population, were estimated to need humanitarian assistance, with 12.1 million of these in acute need.
* The protracted conflict has triggered a severe economic crisis which deepened significantly in 2021, with severe impact on the lives and livelihoods of the Yemeni population.
* By June 2021, WFP had resumed monthly assistance to 10 million people in nine governorates facing the highest food insecurity levels.
* Funding shortfalls had forced WFP to reduce the assistance provided to millions of people by the end of the year.
**Economic Indicators Mentioned:**
* Economic crisis
* Currency depreciation
* Food prices
* Exchange rate


**Publication:** WFP Yemen Annual Country Report 2020
**Publication Date:** 2021
**URL:** https://wfp.tind.io/record/128008/files/ELR%202113.pdf
**Key Findings:**
* Reached around 15 million total beneficiaries in 2020.
* 13 million received Unconditional Resource Transfers (general food assistance, GFA), but beneficiaries in areas under the Sana’a-based authorities received assistance every other month from April onwards.
* The operating environment became more challenging and complex and funding decreased, affecting WFP’s ability to deliver a response commensurate to the reported needs.
* A global shortage of specialized nutritious foods due to COVID-19 impacted the delivery of malnutrition prevention activities for pregnant and lactating women (PLW) from August onwards.
* The scale of WFP’s resilience-building activities doubled compared to 2019, with around 500,000 moderately food insecure people receiving food assistance for assets (FFA), food assistance for training (FFT) and livelihoods support across the country.
**Economic Indicators Mentioned:**
* Economic deterioration
* Purchasing power
* Exchange rate volatility
* Inflation
* Global economic downturn
