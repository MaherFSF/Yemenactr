# Chatham House Yemen Economy Research



## Conflict Economies in the Middle East and North Africa

*   **Publication Date:** 25 June 2019
*   **URL:** https://www.chathamhouse.org/2019/06/conflict-economies-middle-east-and-north-africa

*   **Key Findings:** Economic motivations are key to understanding the war in Yemen. The report identifies three types of conflict sub-economies: capital cities, transit areas/borderlands, and oil-rich areas. Rents in these areas are a major source of funding for armed groups. The report highlights the importance of understanding these local economic dynamics to find a path to peace.
*   **Economic Indicators:** Rents, taxation, arbitrage, oil sector.

## Why isolating the Houthis was a strategic mistake

*   **Publication Date:** 5 November 2021
*   **URL:** https://www.chathamhouse.org/2021/11/why-isolating-houthis-was-strategic-mistake
*   **Key Findings:** The article argues that the international community's decision to isolate the Houthis in Yemen has been a strategic mistake. It has allowed the Houthis to become more radical, build an economic empire, and strengthen their relationship with Iran. The author suggests that the isolation has also had a devastating impact on Yemeni society, leading to the disappearance of the middle class and a lack of free-thinking culture.
*   **Economic Indicators:** The article mentions that the siege has allowed the Houthis to build an economic empire to fuel the war.


## Review: The agony of Yemen’s ‘forgotten’ war

*   **Publication Date:** 2 December 2022
*   **URL:** https://www.chathamhouse.org/publications/the-world-today/2022-12/review-agony-yemens-forgotten-war
*   **Key Findings:** This article reviews the book 'What Have You Left Behind?' by Bushra al-Maqtari. The book is an oral history of the early years of the conflict in Yemen, focusing on the suffering of the Yemeni people. The article notes that the war is often 'forgotten' because Yemen has few hydrocarbon resources and few Yemeni refugees head to Europe.
*   **Economic Indicators:** Hydro-carbon resources.


## The Houthis won’t back down after US and UK strikes on Yemen

*   **Publication Date:** 12 January 2024
*   **URL:** https://www.chathamhouse.org/2024/01/houthis-wont-back-down-after-us-and-uk-strikes-yemen
*   **Key Findings:** The article argues that US and UK air strikes on Yemen will not deter the Houthis from attacking ships in the Red Sea. Instead, the strikes will likely lead to an escalation of the conflict and further destabilize the region. The author notes that the Houthis are more prepared and better equipped than many in the West realize, and that they have a strong incentive to continue their attacks in order to show solidarity with the Palestinians. The article also points out that the air strikes will have a negative impact on the humanitarian situation in Yemen, which is already dire.
*   **Economic Indicators:** The article mentions that shipping firms have seen costs rise during the last months of Houthi attacks. It also notes that the airstrikes will have a tremendously negative impact on food security and transport in the country.
