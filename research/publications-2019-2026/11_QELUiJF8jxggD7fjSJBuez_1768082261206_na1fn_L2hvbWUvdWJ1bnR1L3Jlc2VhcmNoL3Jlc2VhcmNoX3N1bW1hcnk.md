Title: Demographic and Labour Market Trends in Yemen
Date: 31 March 2015
URL: https://www.ilo.org/publications/demographic-and-labour-market-trends-yemen
Key Findings:
- The Yemeni economy needs to create 150,000 jobs a year to maintain an employment-to-population ratio of 75%.
- The economy is struggling to absorb the increasing number of new entrants into the labour market.
- Protection of Yemeni workers in GCC countries and the advancement of women’s economic empowerment are important.

Economic Indicators: Employment-to-population ratio

Title: Yemen - ILOSTAT Country Profile
Date: Latest household survey: Labour Force Survey, 2014, 2014Q4
URL: https://ilostat.ilo.org/data/country-profiles/yem/
Key Findings:
- This profile presents selected labour market data, typically for the latest year available, covering a wide range of key aspects such as labour force participation, employment, labour underutilization, working conditions, social protection, and occupational safety and health.
- The data contributes to monitoring global efforts to achieve SDG 1 (No Poverty), SDG 5 (Gender Equality), SDG 8 (Decent Work and Economic Growth), and SDG 10 (Reduced Inequality).

Economic Indicators: Working poverty rate, Youth NEET rate, Gender pay gap, Social protection coverage, Share of women in management positions, Unemployment rate, Child labour rate, Informal employment rate, Labour income share.

Title: Navigating the digital and artificial intelligence revolution in Arab labour markets
Date: 22 September 2025
URL: https://www.ilo.org/publications/navigating-digital-and-artificial-intelligence-revolution-arab-labour
Key Findings:
- The risk of widespread job loss from technologies like generative AI is limited, but the opportunity for job enhancement is vast.
- The transformation could exacerbate existing inequalities, especially for women and older workers.
- The report recommends proactive, inclusive policies to ensure a just transition that creates more and better-quality jobs for all.

Economic Indicators: None mentioned
Title: Facilitating transition between social assistance and social insurance
Date: 25 February 2025
URL: https://www.ilo.org/publications/facilitating-transition-between-social-assistance-and-social-insurance
Key Findings:
- The report provides a conceptual framework and policy recommendations for ensuring better transitions between social assistance and employment-related social insurance in the Arab Region.
- It identifies four critical factors: social assistance design features, social insurance design features, labour market support measures, and financial incentives for joining social insurance.

Economic Indicators: None mentioned
