# CBY Aden Exchange Companies Regulation Campaign 2025

**Source:** Yemen Monitor, January 2, 2026
**URL:** https://www.yemenmonitor.com/en/Details/ArtMID/908/ArticleID/160160

## Overview

The Central Bank of Yemen (Aden) launched a wide-ranging supervisory campaign in July 2025 to regulate the exchange market and enhance financial stability. By the end of December 2025, approximately 79 exchange companies and institutions had their licenses suspended or revoked.

## Key Decisions

### January 2, 2026 Decision
Governor Ahmed Ghaleb issued two decisions:

**Decision 1 - License Suspensions:**
- Al-Bal'asi Exchange Company
- Al-Khader Exchange Company
- Suhail Exchange Establishment
- All premises ordered closed effective immediately

**Decision 2 - License Revocation:**
- Al-Shamil Exchange Company (Mansoura district branch)

## Legal Basis
- Laws regulating exchange operations
- Anti-money laundering regulations
- Counter-terrorism financing laws
- Public interest considerations

## Campaign Timeline

| Date | Action | Details |
|------|--------|---------|
| July 2025 | Campaign Launch | CBY Aden begins supervisory campaign |
| July-Dec 2025 | Ongoing Enforcement | Field supervisory reports and inspections |
| December 2025 | Total Suspended/Revoked | ~79 exchange companies |
| January 2, 2026 | Latest Decision | 4 more companies affected |

## Context

This campaign is part of broader FX management measures implemented by the IRG in 2025:
- National Committee for Regulation and Financing of Imports (NCRFI) established July 2025
- Limits on foreign currency exchanges
- Prohibition on using foreign currency for local transactions
- Targeting currency manipulation and speculation

## Impact
- Exchange rate stabilized from peak of 2,905 YER/USD (July 2025) to ~1,676 YER/USD (August 2025)
- Reduced currency speculation
- Improved formal banking sector FX flows
- Welcomed by private sector and humanitarian organizations (per IMF)
