# Aden Refinery Company Research Data

## Source: Official Website (arc-ye.com) + Search Results
URL: https://www.arc-ye.com/

### Company Overview
- **Name:** Aden Refinery Company (ARC) - شركة مصافي عدن
- **Original Name:** British Petroleum Company
- **Founded:** November 1, 1952 (groundwork started)
- **Inaugurated:** July 1954 (by Queen Elizabeth II)
- **Nationalized:** May 1977 (handed over by BP to Yemen government)
- **Legal Basis:** Law number 15 of 1977

### Executive Leadership
- **Executive Director:** Eng. Ahmed Musaed Saeed

### Location
- **Headquarters:** Little Aden (115), Aden, Republic of Yemen
- **P.O. Box:** 3003
- **Fax:** +967 02 376600/1

### Production Capacity
- **Original design capacity:** 5 million tons/year
- **Upgraded capacity (1960s):** 8 million tons/year
- **Current capacity:** 110,000-120,000 barrels per day (bbl/d)
- **Equivalent:** ~19,000 m³/d

### Infrastructure
- 19-mile pipeline to Aden harbor bunkering facilities
- Connected to Aden Port for ship bunkering services

### Business Activities
1. Processing crude oil for local market petroleum products
2. Storage of crude oil and petroleum products for third parties
3. Trading petroleum products
4. Selling bunker fuel to ships (direct and indirect)

### Products
- Petroleum products for domestic market
- Household cooking gas (LPG)
- Road asphalt
- Bunker fuels for ships

### Strategic Importance
- Largest industrial establishment in Republic of Yemen (by annual income)
- Sole leading refinery in Yemen
- Strategic location linking world continents
- Hub for ship bunkering on major shipping routes

### Operational Status
- Suffered significant damage during 2015 war
- Operations suspended for extended period
- Reopening efforts underway (as of 2024-2025)
- Approved to operate as "free zone" (July 2024)

### Historical Significance
- Over six decades of refining industry operations
- One of the oldest refineries in the Arabian Peninsula

### Employee Count (from search results)
- Estimated: 51-200 employees (ZoomInfo data)

### Recent Developments (2025)
- President Al-Zubaidi inspecting restart operations
- Free Zone Authority completing approval procedures
