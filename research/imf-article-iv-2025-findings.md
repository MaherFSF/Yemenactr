# IMF Article IV Consultation 2025 - Key Findings

**Source:** IMF Mission Concluding Statement, October 9, 2025
**URL:** https://www.imf.org/en/news/articles/2025/10/09/imf-cs-yemen-2025-imf-article-iv-mission

## Overview
- First Article IV consultation in 11 years (since 2014)
- Conflict halted Extended Credit Facility (ECF) arrangement
- Renewed consultation reflects enhanced institutional capacity

## Key Economic Indicators (2024-2025)

### GDP
- Real GDP contracted ~27% over past decade
- 2024: GDP contracted 1.5% (third consecutive year)
- 2025 projection: -0.5% contraction
- 2026 projection: +0.5% growth
- 2030 projection: +2.5% growth

### Inflation
- 2024: 27%
- July 2025: Above 35% YoY
- Rial depreciated 30% since beginning of 2025
- Expected to ease after August 2025 FX measures

### Government Finances
- Revenue declined from 22.5% of GDP (2014) to below 12% (2024)
- Public debt: Over 100% of IRG GDP
- Deficit: 1.9% of GDP in 2024 (down from 12%+ in 2022)
- Saudi Arabia provided ~$2 billion support (2023-24)

### Current Account
- 2014: -2.1% of GDP
- 2022: -40.6% of GDP
- 2023-24 average: -14.5% of GDP
- Improvement due to import compression, remittances, grants

### Foreign Reserves
- Less than one month of import coverage
- Critically low levels

## Key Policy Developments

### FX Management (2025)
- July 2025: National Committee for Regulation and Financing of Imports (NCRFI) established
- Limits on foreign currency exchanges
- Prohibition on using foreign currency for local transactions
- Revocation of licenses for money exchangers suspected of manipulation
- Result: Rial appreciation and stabilization

### Fiscal Adjustment
- Revenue fell 8+ percentage points of GDP since 2022
- Causes: Halted oil exports, trade shift to northern ports, smuggling, governorate revenue retention
- Spending reduced 5.4 percentage points of GDP
- CBY sterilized Treasury overdrafts using Saudi deposits

### Banking Sector
- Major banks relocated headquarters to Aden after US FTO designation of Houthis (early 2025)
- Safeguarding correspondent banking relationships
- Need for improved bank supervision and KYC practices

## Revenue Challenges
1. Halted oil exports (since 2022 Houthi attacks)
2. Trade shifting to northern ports
3. Rising smuggling
4. Governorates retaining central government revenue
5. Competition among governorates for port traffic
6. Differing tax rates and customs tariffs

## IMF Recommendations

### Fiscal
- Link expenditure authorizations to timely revenue remittances
- Improve port oversight
- Unify and remit taxes/customs by governorates
- Value customs at market exchange rates
- Update customs duties
- Implement Short-Term Emergency Revenue Plan

### Monetary
- Discontinue monetary financing for price stability
- Pursue market-determined exchange rate
- Ensure NCRFI rate aligns with market rate

### Financial Sector
- Expand oversight to all deposit-taking institutions
- Improve bank supervision
- Strengthen KYC practices
- Monitor Houthi-linked transactions

### Structural
- Strengthen governance institutions
- Implement AML/CFT policies
- Treasury single account
- Reduce barriers to business
- Electricity sector reforms
- Renewable energy development

## External Support
- September 2025: $368 million Saudi financing package announced
- Budget, energy, and health support
- UAE also providing support
- Comprehensive creditor negotiations needed for debt sustainability
