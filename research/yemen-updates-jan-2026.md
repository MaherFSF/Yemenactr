# Yemen Economic Updates - January 2026

## Critical Events (December 2025 - January 2026)

### Central Bank of Yemen - Aden (CBY Aden)

**Governor:** Ahmed Ghaleb (since 2023)

**January 9, 2026 - Board Meeting:**
- First board meeting of 2026 held at CBY headquarters in Aden
- Reviewed precautionary measures taken during recent security developments
- Approved 2025 audit contract to international firm with high ranking
- Reviewed 2025 achievements and proposed 2026 action plan
- Directed Planning Committee to review plans in light of reform programs

**Exchange Market Regulation Campaign (July 2025 - December 2025):**
- Wide-ranging supervisory campaign launched since July 2025
- 79 exchange companies/institutions had licenses suspended or revoked by end of December 2025
- Latest suspensions (January 2, 2026):
  - Al-Bal'asi Exchange Company - license suspended
  - Al-Khader Exchange Company - license suspended
  - Suhail Exchange Establishment - license suspended
  - Al-Shamil Exchange Company (Mansoura branch) - license revoked
- Actions based on anti-money laundering and counter-terrorism financing laws

### Southern Transitional Council (STC) Crisis

**Timeline:**
- December 2025: STC expanded control over non-Houthi controlled Yemen
- Early January 2026: Armed clashes between Arab coalition forces and STC
- January 7, 2026: Saudi-led coalition bombed Shabwa (al-Zubaidi's home province) after he skipped talks
- January 8, 2026: Aidarus al-Zubaidi fled to UAE
- January 9, 2026: STC announced dissolution of all political, executive and organizational bodies
- January 10, 2026: Thousands rally in Aden in support of STC (disputed dissolution)

**Government Response:**
- "Nation's Shield" forces took control of vital facilities in Aden
- Instructions to CBY Aden to freeze bank accounts linked to al-Zubaidi and senior STC figures
- Minister of Defense dismissed following security events in Hadramawt and Al-Mahra
- Arrangements to hand over security of Al-Mahra ports to "Nation's Shield" forces

### Exchange Rates (January 2026)

**Aden (IRG-controlled areas):**
- Approximately 1,890-2,050 YER/USD (parallel market)
- Significant divergence from Sanaa rate

**Sanaa (Houthi-controlled areas):**
- Approximately 530-600 YER/USD
- More stable due to currency controls

### Humanitarian Situation

**UN Report (January 4, 2026):**
- Yemen aid response "buckling under funding cuts"
- Needs continue to rise
- Fragile humanitarian response being strained

### IMF Engagement

**December 2025:**
- IMF published report on "Customs Reform and Emergency Revenue Mobilization"
- Focus on improving operational efficiency, transparency, stakeholder engagement
- Supporting Yemen's fiscal stability
