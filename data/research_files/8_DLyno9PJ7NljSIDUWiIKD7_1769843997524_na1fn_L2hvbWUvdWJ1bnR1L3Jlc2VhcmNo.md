## UNICEF Yemen Humanitarian Situation Report (Jan-Dec 2023)

- **Source:** [Yemen Humanitarian Situation Report No. 4 (January-December 2023)](https://www.unicef.org/media/154716/file/Yemen-Humanitarian-SitRep-December-2023.pdf)

### Key Findings (2023)

*   **Humanitarian Need:**
    *   21.6 million people, including 11.1 million children, required humanitarian assistance.
    *   4.5 million people are internally displaced (since 2015).
*   **Health & Nutrition:**
    *   1,154,919 children vaccinated against measles and rubella.
    *   4,679,857 children under five screened for malnutrition.
    *   53,000+ suspected cases of measles (2,347 confirmed).
    *   1,978 suspected cases of diphtheria.
    *   3 new confirmed cases of polio (total 239 since Nov 2021).
    *   8,425 suspected cholera cases.
*   **Water, Sanitation and Hygiene (WASH):**
    *   2,030,283 people reached with safe drinking water.
    *   15.3 million people (7.8 million children) lack access to safe water and WASH services.
*   **Education:**
    *   1,233,177 children received learning materials.
    *   8.6 million children required educational assistance (2.7 million out-of-school).
*   **Child Protection:**
    *   845,094 people received explosive ordnance risk education.
    *   9 million children required child protection services.
*   **Displacement:**
    *   313,056 newly displaced people received Rapid Response Mechanism (RRM) kits.
    *   319,445 people were newly displaced due to conflict and climate events.
*   **Food Security:**
    *   17.3 million people experienced high levels of food insecurity.


## UNICEF Yemen Humanitarian Situation Report (Jan-June 2023)

- **Source:** [Yemen Humanitarian Situation Report No. 2 (January-June 2023)](https://www.unicef.org/media/144446/file/Yemen-Humanitarian-SitRep-MidYear-2023.pdf)

### Key Findings (First Half of 2023)

*   **Humanitarian Need:**
    *   21.6 million people, including 11.1 million children, in need of humanitarian assistance.
    *   3.1 million internally displaced people (since 2015).
*   **Health & Nutrition:**
    *   1,224,153 children under five vaccinated with tOPV.
    *   2,847,322 children under five screened for malnutrition.
    *   160,603 children with Severe Acute Malnutrition (SAM) admitted for treatment.
    *   28,063 suspected cases of measles and rubella (1,554 confirmed), with 342 associated deaths.
    *   227 confirmed cases of polio since November 2021.
    *   3,878 cases of AWD/suspected cholera, with 4 associated deaths.
*   **Child Protection:**
    *   184,386 people received psychosocial support.
*   **Education:**
    *   Monthly cash incentives provided to 34,830 teachers and school-based staff.
*   **Displacement:**
    *   108,000 individuals (15,450 households) affected by conflict and extreme weather.
*   **Food Security:**
    *   An estimated 500,000 children are acutely malnourished, including 100,000 with severe acute malnutrition.
    *   Up to a quarter million pregnant and lactating women and girls are acutely malnourished.



## UNICEF Yemen Consolidated Emergency Report (2022)

- **Source:** [Yemen Consolidated Emergency Report 2022](https://open.unicef.org/sites/transparency/files/2023-05/Yemen%20CER%202022.pdf)

### Key Findings (2022)

*   **Humanitarian Need:**
    *   23.4 million people in need of humanitarian assistance (increase from 20.7 million in 2021).
    *   12.9 million children in need of humanitarian assistance.
    *   4.3 million people internally displaced.
*   **Health & Nutrition:**
    *   2.2 million children under five are acutely malnourished, with 538,000 suffering from severe acute malnutrition (SAM).
    *   1.3 million pregnant and lactating women are malnourished.
    *   Outbreaks of measles, diphtheria, and polio continued.
*   **Water, Sanitation and Hygiene (WASH):**
    *   17.8 million people lack access to safe water and adequate sanitation services.
*   **Education:**
    *   2 million school-aged children are out of school.
*   **Child Protection:**
    *   Over 11,000 children have been killed or maimed since the conflict started.
    *   4,000 children have been recruited into the fighting.


## UNICEF Yemen Consolidated Emergency Report (2021)

- **Source:** [Yemen Consolidated Emergency Report 2021](https://open.unicef.org/sites/transparency/files/2022-04/Yemen%20CER%202021.pdf)

### Key Findings (2021)

*   **Humanitarian Need:**
    *   20.7 million people in need of humanitarian assistance, including 11.3 million children.
    *   4 million people internally displaced, including 2 million children.
*   **Health & Nutrition:**
    *   2.3 million children under five were projected to suffer from acute malnutrition, with 400,000 at risk of death from severe acute malnutrition (SAM).
    *   1.2 million pregnant or breastfeeding women were acutely malnourished.
    *   Outbreaks of measles, diphtheria, and polio continued.
*   **Water, Sanitation and Hygiene (WASH):**
    *   15.4 million people required support to access WASH services.
*   **Education:**
    *   2 million children were out of school.
    *   Over 2,500 schools were destroyed, damaged, or used for non-educational purposes.
*   **Child Protection:**
    *   10,200 children had been killed or maimed since the beginning of the conflict.
    *   3,500 children had been recruited into the fighting.


## UNICEF Yemen Humanitarian Situation Report (End of Year 2020)

- **Source:** [Yemen Humanitarian Situation Report, End of Year 2020](https://www.unicef.org/media/93126/file/Yemen%20Humanitarian%20Situation%20Report,%20End%20of%20Year%202020.pdf)

### Key Findings (2020)

*   **Humanitarian Need:**
    *   24.3 million people in need of humanitarian assistance, including 12.2 million children.
    *   4.4 million internally displaced persons (IDPs), including 1.71 million children.
*   **Health & Nutrition:**
    *   3.6 million children under 5 screened for malnutrition.
    *   231,062 children under 5 with severe acute malnutrition (SAM) treated.
    *   7.2 million children vaccinated in two rounds of polio campaigns in response to a cVDPV1 outbreak.
*   **COVID-19:**
    *   2,101 officially confirmed cases, with 610 associated deaths (a 29% case fatality rate).
*   **Child Protection:**
    *   In December 2020 alone, 2 children were killed and 30 were maimed.
