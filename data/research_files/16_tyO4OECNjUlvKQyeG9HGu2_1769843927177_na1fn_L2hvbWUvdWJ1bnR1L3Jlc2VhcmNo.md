# ESCWA Yemen Economic Data Research

This document contains the findings of the research on Yemen's economic data from ESCWA for the period 2010-2026.


## Extracted Data from Reports

### Macroeconomic Data

*   **GDP:** Contracted by more than 50% since 2015. Declined by 2% in 2023 and an estimated 1% in 2024.
*   **GDP per capita:** Approximately $541 in 2023, a cumulative decline of 54%.
*   **Poverty:** 74% of the population lived below the poverty line.

### Humanitarian Data

*   **Youth Mortality (15-24 years):** 22 per 1,000 people.
*   **Education:** A child starting school at age 4 is projected to complete only 7.9 years of schooling by age 18.
*   **Child Labor:** 19% of families have sent their children to work.
*   **Health Infrastructure:** Only 53% of hospitals are operational.
*   **Mental Health:** An estimated 8 million people require mental health and psychosocial support.
*   **Human Development Index (HDI):** Ranked 186 out of 191 countries in 2022 (down from 160 in 2014).
*   **Life Expectancy:** Decreased from 67.4 years in 2014 to 63.7 years in 2022.
*   **Displacement:** 4,560,000 internally displaced persons in 2024.
*   **Food Security:** 17 million people (half the population) facing food insecurity (Nov 2023 - Oct 2024). 62% of households reported insufficient food consumption in July 2024.
*   **Malnutrition (2023/2024):** Wasting: 17%, Stunting: 49%, Underweight: 41%.
*   **Humanitarian Funding:** Only 27.9% of the UN Yemen Humanitarian Response Plan was funded as of September 9, 2024.

### Social Data

*   **Gender Gap:** Yemen has ranked last in the Global Gender Gap Index since 1998.
